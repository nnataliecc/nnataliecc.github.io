  <section id="overview">
    <h2>Project Overview</h2>
    <p>
      This project analyzes flow cytometry data from the LSDS-122 dataset (Krukowski et al., 2021), 
      examining immune cell populations in blood samples. The analysis focuses on T-cell markers 
      (CD3+, CD4+, CD8+) and monocytes, with particular attention to the CD4/CD8 ratio as an 
      indicator of immune system balance.
    </p>

    <div class="info-box">
      <h4>Research Question</h4>
      <p>How do immune system changes in spaceflight environments affect astronaut mental health and cognitive function?</p>
    </div>

    <h3>Dataset Information</h3>
    <p>
      <strong>Source:</strong> NASA Life Sciences Data Archive (LSDS-122)<br>
      <strong>Citation:</strong> Krukowski, K., et al. (2021)<br>
      <strong>Samples:</strong> Tail vein blood samples from rodent spaceflight analog<br>
      <strong>Measurements:</strong> T-cell populations, monocytes, immune ratios
    </p>

    <h3>Key Analyses</h3>
    <div class="info-box">
      <ul>
        <li>Distribution analysis of immune cell percentages</li>
        <li>Correlation patterns between different cell types</li>
        <li>CD4/CD8 ratio trends across samples</li>
        <li>Statistical comparison using box plots and violin plots</li>
        <li>3D visualization of multi-variable relationships</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </section>
